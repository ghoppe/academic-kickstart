Notes for Philip Mattoon
Philip Mattoon, a soldier who had fought in the Narragansett Campaign, King Philip's War, came from Ipswich, Mass. to Hadley, Mass. with Captain William Turner and his company in the spring of 1676, to assist in the Western Massachusetts defense against Indian attacks. He was living in Hadley, Mass. when he participated in the Falls Fight, May 19, 1676. He settled at Springfield, Mass. where he married on September 10, 1677 Sarah Hawks, daughter of John and Elizabeth Hawks of Hadley, who was born at Windsor, Conn., Sept. 29, 1657. The probable date of birth of Philip Mattoon was 1655.
At Springfield five children were born. In 1686 the family removed to Deerfield, Mass. and there five more children were born, the last child, Mary, posthumously.
In 1678 Philip Mattoon leased for eleven years all the lands that Major Pynchon had at Pocumtuck (Deerfield). For part pay he agreed to build and leave a house 30 x 20 feet, 10 foot stud; also, a barn 24 x 48 feet, 14 foot stud. He was a part of the permanent settlement in Deerfield and built on Lot No. 22. This was held by Philip, and by his heirs, as late as 1714. At his death, December 30, 1696, the house and lot were appraised at £20. His widow married, second, Daniel Belding of Deerfield, whose first wife was killed by the Indians in 1696 and his second in 1704, he himself having been taken at one time as a captive to Canada. Sarah survived him 20 years and died on September 15, 1751, aged 94, this being 74 years after her first marriage.
