Notes for Wilfred Joseph “Dina” Denomme
Wilfred Joseph Denomme
Wilfred Joseph Denomme (Dina) of Goderich passed away at London Health Science Centre, University Campus, London. He was 78.
He was a retired florist and World War II veteran R.C.N. He was born in Hay Township, moved to Clinton at a young age and attended Clinton Public School and Clinton High School. He worked at Cookes greenhouse in Clinton before joining the navy during World War II. After the war, he married Edna L. MacDonald and returned to Cookes Florist to work. In 1957 he moved with his family to Goderich to open the Denomme Flower Shop on The Square until retiring in 1987.
Wilfred was a Past President of Branch 140 Clinton Legion, and Branch 109 Goderich Legion. He was a 4th Degree Knight in the Knights of Columbus, Father Nagle Council, No 5420, Goderich. He was also the past president of the Goderich Kinsmen Club, a member of the Menesetung Canoe Club and the Goderich Duplicate Bridge Club.
Wilfred is survived by his wife Edna of 57 years, his daughters Mary Lou Durnin (Allan), Audrey Smith (Brian), Rosanne Yeats (Graham), Beverly OBrien (Tim), Patricia Chisholm (Mark) and his son Wilfred F. (Will)Denomme (Joanne), all of Goderich.
He is survived by his grandchildren Naomi Lou Bester, Holly and Dan Smith; Brendan and Vanessa Yeats; Danielle, Denise and Patrick OBrien; Jesse Baechler and Jamie Lee Chisholm; Justine, Jasmine and LucasDenomme; and great-granddaughter Ella Lu Bester.
He is also survived by his brothers Clarence (Peggy) and Donald (Cis), of Goderich; and his sisters Geraldine Mattoon of Nipawin, Sask.; and Bernice Moore (Eric) of Goderich; as well as his brother-in-law Gordon MacDonald, (Vera) of Clinton and sister-in-law Joan Denomme of North Bay.
He was predeceased by his parents Lawrence and Verda (Rau) Denomme of Clinton, brother-in-law Glen Mattoon of Nipawin, Sask., sister Cecelia McNeil and Colin of Goderich, brother Frances, North Bay, John Paul (Anita) of Hamilton; and brother-in-law Fred MacDonald (Norah) of Clinton. He is also survived by many nieces and nephews.
Funeral mass was celebrated at St. Peters Catholic Church, Goderich on Monday, Oct. 6. Rev. Leo Ramer was the celebrant.
Pallbearers were Allan Durnin, Brian Smith, Graham Yeats, Tim OBrien and Mark Chisholm.
Interment Clinton Cemetery.
