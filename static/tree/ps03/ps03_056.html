Notes for Marin Boucher
REFN: 19 The following information is taken from an article published in Fren ch Canadian and Acadian Genealogical Review in 1968 From the Province of Perch e to New France, and From the Province of Quebec to New England by Adrien L. R inguette There are 3 Boucher and related families who emigrated to New France in 1634, 1635 and 1638, respectively, from Mortagne. The Boucher families ar e covered by Godbout, in Vieilles Familles de France en Nouvelle-France (1976) . The family of Marin Boucher (1588-1671) accompanied Robert Giffard to New Fr ance in 1634. Marin Boucher was from the parish of St-Jean de Mortagne, and, like Jean Guyon, he was a mason. It was his house that Jean Guyon bought afte r settling in the parish of St. Jean in 1615. Marin Boucher had married, on 7 Feb 1611, Juliane Baril, at St-Langis le Mortagne. The marriage took place th e year following the death of her father, Jean Baril, a merchant of St-Langis , and the young couple settled in St-Langis, where they had 7 children, only o ne of which was to accompany Marin Boucher to New France in 1634. Juliane Bar il died in 1627, and two years later Marin Boucher married Perinne Malet, from the town of Courgeoust. This couple had two children born in France and 5 bo rn in Canada, making 14 children in all for Marin Boucher. Marin Boucher and P erinne Mallet brought 3 children to New France. They settled in Beauport initi ally, where Marin Boucher was associated for a time with his brother-in-law T homas Hayot. However, the Bourdon map of 1641 shows that Marin Boucher and his sons were already located in Chateau-Richer, formal title to their property t o come later. He divided his property among his sons and sons-in-law. In the 1 667 census he stated that he was 80 years old, and he died several years late r.
