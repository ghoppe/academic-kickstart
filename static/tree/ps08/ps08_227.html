Notes for Gilbert Desautels
REFN: 2536 Antoine de la Mothe de Cadillac, dit - le Marquis de Jourdis, had served the King of France long and well. He had crossed the ocean many times, had helped take Port Royal and had been appointed the commandant at Michilimac kinac in the northern peninsula of Michigan. He had no qualms about trading fi re water for Indian furs, but the Jesuits protested to King Louis that Cadill ac was destroying the morals of their converts. Cadillac was removed but allow ed to build a fort at 'the city of the strait' now called Detroit- in July of 1701. A church and stockade were built and a place of habitation for his men. He returned to Montreal to get supplies and equipment to build a city. He nee ded many men to transport the goods up to Detroit. One who volunteered was Gil bert Desautels, youngest son of Pierre the Pioneer. He arrived in Detroit on M ay 30, 1705 with his barge of goods. He is listed among the census reports of Detroit - 1701 - 1710. It is unknown how long he remained. Cadillac urged the young men to marry Indian maidens but Gilbert returned to Montreal where he m arried Marie Charlotte Etienne on January 30, 1708. Of all the early Desautel s family he had the largest family-19 children. I am descended from the 18th: Joseph born 8, 1728, who in turn married Marie Bouteiller in 1745.
